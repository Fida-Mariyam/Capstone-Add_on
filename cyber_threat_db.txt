=== THREAT CATEGORY: BANKING KYC & ACCOUNT BLOCK PHISHING ===
IDENTIFIERS: "KYC expired", "Account suspended", "PAN card update", "Click here to unblock", fake bank links, alphanumeric sender IDs, verify requests, update KYC details.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Extreme urgency, fear of immediate financial loss or account penalties.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Contact your bank or financial institution directly using trusted phone numbers from their official website to verify the request.
2. Check with your branch to understand valid, authorised methods for updating KYC data.
3. Report any suspicious messages or communication to your bank immediately.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Never click on unverified, suspicious links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email.
2. Never share secure credentials including NetBanking login IDs, passwords, card details, PINs, or transaction OTPs on any website or app.
3. Do not share physical or digital copies of identity documents (Aadhaar, PAN) with unknown individuals.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call the national cyber fraud helpline at 1930 or file a complaint online at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: PHISHING, VISHING, AND QUISHING SCAMS ===
IDENTIFIERS: Suspicious links, fake email senders, caller ID spoofing, unknown international numbers, voice phishing, "Scan QR code to receive money", embedded payment app codes.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Authority impersonation (pretending to be bank staff or police), synthetic trust, false rewards.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Hover over hyperlinks in emails to inspect the real destination URL and spot character typos.
2. Install reliable spam blockers, password-protect your voicemail, and keep device software updated.
3. Verify website safety by checking for the HTTPS protocol and the security padlock icon before typing info.
4. Only scan QR codes displayed on official portals or inside physical shops of verified businesses.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Never click on links inside unexpected messages; delete spam texts from unknown senders immediately.
2. Never scan a QR code to 'receive' or 'redeem' money-legitimate incoming transfers never require scanning.
3. Never share passwords, usernames, or OTPs over a phone call; genuine institutions will never ask for them.
4. Do not trust your phone's caller ID display blindly, as fraudsters frequently spoof official numbers.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call 1930 or lodge an official report on the portal at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: COURIER EXTORTION & FAKE DIGITAL ARREST ===
IDENTIFIERS: "Seized illegal package", "FedEx contraband drugs", "Customs department alert", WhatsApp or Skype video call interrogation, "Digital Arrest execution", CBI/Police impersonation.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Severe legal intimidation, fear of public shame, manufactured legal authority.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Remain calm and understand that police, narcotics, or customs officials never conduct criminal interrogations over video calls.
2. Know your rights: There is absolutely no legal process called "digital arrest" in Indian law.
3. Disconnect suspicious video calls immediately and report the caller account details using the "Report Suspect Tab" on the national portal.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Do not share any personal details, home addresses, or financial information during an unsolicited video call.
2. Do not transfer funds to any "safe government escrow account" to clear your name or avoid arrest.
3. Do not remain isolated on a long, continuous video call; scammers use prolonged engagement to trap you.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call 1930 or submit details under the "Report Suspect Tab" at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: DEEPFAKE CYBERCRIME & SOCIAL MEDIA IMPERSONATION ===
IDENTIFIERS: Fake video clips, manipulated audio recordings, cloned voice notes, social media accounts mimicking friends or family, unverified fund requests via chat.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Emotional manipulation, urgent cries for help, exploiting trust in family or public figures.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Verify the identity of any friend or relative requesting emergency money by calling them on a known, offline phone number.
2. Check social media profiles for blue verification checkmarks, consistent handles, and historical posts.
3. Report fake, cloned profiles directly to the social media platform's security team.
4. Carefully analyze media content for unnatural lip movements, blinking anomalies, or voice distortions.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Do not make immediate online payments to online charities or acquaintances without visual or vocal verification.
2. Do not share, forward, or spread exaggerated, emotional videos without verifying their actual source.
3. Do not leave your social media profiles completely public; restrict privacy settings to protect personal photos from being scraped.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call 1930 or log a case online at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: TECHNICAL SUPPORT & REMOTE ACCESS FRAUD ===
IDENTIFIERS: "PC infected", "Virus warning popup", "Microsoft Support helpline", screen-sharing software, AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport, remote device access.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Panic, technical jargon confusion, urgency to fix a fabricated security issue.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Confirm a caller's identity independently through trusted, public customer care channels.
2. Log out of all mobile banking and UPI payment apps before downloading any shared support tools.
3. Completely uninstall remote screen-sharing software as soon as a verified technical task is finished.
4. Set strong, complex device passwords and mandate multi-factor authentication (2FA) across profiles.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Never grant remote device access or screen control to unverified individuals or unexpected cold callers.
2. Do not download or install unfamiliar utilities from third-party links sent via text or email.
3. Do not type passwords, login credentials, or sensitive bank details while your screen is actively being mirrored.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Cut internet access immediately, call 1930, or report at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: ONLINE JOB, CYBER SLAVERY, & TASK FRAUD ===
IDENTIFIERS: "Earn money by liking YouTube videos", "Google Maps rating job", high pay for easy home tasks, Telegram group assignments, fake international job offers, tourist visa work.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Greed, low-effort reward traps, coercive financial dependency, building initial trust with tiny payouts.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Verify business listings, job details, and employer credentials using official corporate directories and newspapers.
2. Only process international job placements through government-approved, verified employment agents.
3. Ask highly specific, technical questions during interviews to cross-examine online recruiters.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Never pay upfront fees, registration deposits, or "processing charges" to secure any job or unlock commissions.
2. Do not apply for random employment openings advertised inside unverified social media groups or messaging apps.
3. Never accept an overseas job offer if they instruct you to travel and work on a basic tourist visa.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call 1930 or flag human trafficking/forced labor risks on the "Report Suspect Tab" at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: LOTTERY, PONZI, & TRADING INVESTMENT SCAMS ===
IDENTIFIERS: "KBC lottery winner", lucky draw notification, guaranteed 200% returns, high yield with zero risk, social media trading groups, unverified financial apps, fake RBI accounts.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Greed, exploiting financial desperation, creating false consistency in fake investment graphs.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Deal exclusively with SEBI-registered brokers and official regulated banking intermediaries for all market investments.
2. Verify all financial schemes independently through trusted channels of regulated market entities.
3. Stay skeptical and evaluate claims systematically when an unexpected prize or reward notification arrives.
CRITICAL DON'TS:
1. Never send advance processing money, conversion fees, or tax payments to claim a prize or lottery.
2. Do not join or trust dubious Telegram/WhatsApp communities that aggressively promote closed trading applications.
3. Do not share your bank credentials, Aadhaar number, or PAN card with any unverified platform promising wealth.
4. Note that the RBI never holds personal public accounts, manages commercial deposits, or sanctions citizen prizes.
REPORTING HELPLINE: Call 1930 or report financial fraud directly at https://cybercrime.gov.in.

=== THREAT CATEGORY: MALWARE, RANSOMWARE, & INFRASTRUCTURE CYBERATTACKS ===
IDENTIFIERS: Fake banking APK files, locked files, encrypted extensions, "Pay ransom to unlock", juice jacking, public USB port modifications, malicious search ads, fake customer care numbers.
PSYCHOLOGICAL TACTIC: Complete system blockage, functional panic, extortion through data hostage scenarios.
MANDATORY DOS:
1. Download mobile apps strictly from authorized marketplaces like Google Play Store, Apple App Store, or direct bank links.
2. Isolate infected systems instantly by disconnecting network cables and Wi-Fi to stop malware propagation.
